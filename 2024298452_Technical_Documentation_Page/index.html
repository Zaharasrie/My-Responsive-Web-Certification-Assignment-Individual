Introduction to Artificial Intelligence

Artificial Intelligence, commonly known as AI, is a technology that allows computers to perform tasks that normally require human intelligence.

AI systems can analyze information, recognize patterns, solve problems, and make decisions based on data.

AI means creating intelligent systems that can think and learn.
  • Problem solving
  • Decision making
Machine Learning

Machine learning is a branch of AI where computers learn from data without being directly programmed for every task.

Algorithms improve their performance by identifying patterns and making predictions from large datasets.

Model + Data = Learning Process
  • Supervised learning
  • Unsupervised learning
Neural Networks

Neural networks are systems inspired by the human brain and are used to process complex information.

They consist of input layers, hidden layers, and output layers that work together to produce results.

Input → Hidden Layer → Output
AI Applications

AI is widely used in many fields including healthcare, education, finance, and entertainment.

Examples include virtual assistants, recommendation systems, and self-driving vehicles.

AI + Data = Smart Applications
  • Healthcare diagnosis
  • Chatbots and assistants
Future of AI

The future of AI includes more advanced technologies that can improve human life and increase productivity.

However, AI development also raises ethical concerns such as privacy, security, and fairness.

Future AI = Innovation + Responsibility
  • Better automation
  • Responsible AI development
